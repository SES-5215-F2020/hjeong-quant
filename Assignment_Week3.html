correlation1 <- cor.test(tract_data$PINCP, tract_data$JWMNP)

Based on the Pearsons r(or correlation coefficient) and p-values for the correlation tests above, a correlation close to +0 indicates a week linear relationship between the variables. Also, its positive correlation at a 95% confidence level. A p-value lower than the conventional 5% (P<0.05) demonstrates its statistically significance.

I want to see what this relationship looks like, so I chose to represent this on a scatterplot.
